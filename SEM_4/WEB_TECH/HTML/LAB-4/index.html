Welcome to the Taj Mahal



About the Taj Mahal:

The Taj Mahal, located in Agra, India, is an ivory-white marble mausoleum on the banks of the Yamuna River. It was commissioned in 1632 by the Mughal emperor Shah Jahan to house the tomb of his favorite wife, Mumtaz Mahal. The Taj Mahal is considered one of the most beautiful buildings in the world and is a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

History:

Construction of the Taj Mahal began in 1632 and took approximately 20 years to complete. It is said that more than 20,000 artisans and craftsmen were involved in its construction. The Taj Mahal represents the finest example of Mughal architecture, combining elements from Persian, Islamic, and Indian architectural styles.

Architecture:

The Taj Mahal is renowned for its symmetrical design and exquisite craftsmanship. The main mausoleum is flanked by four minarets, each standing at over 40 meters tall. The central dome reaches a height of around 73 meters and is adorned with intricate carvings and calligraphy.

Visiting the Taj Mahal:

Millions of visitors from around the world flock to the Taj Mahal each year to marvel at its beauty. Visitors can explore the mausoleum, gardens, and surrounding structures, learning about its history and significance.
